Brown Bear

"Despite their reputation, most brown bears are not highly carnivorous, as they derive up to 90% of their dietary food energy from vegetable matter. Brown bears often feed on a variety of plant life, including berries, grasses, flowers, acorns and pine cones as well as mosses and fungi such as mushrooms."(Source)

  • Scientific Name: Ursus arctos
  • Average Length: 1.4 to 2.8 meters
  • Average Lifespan: 25 years
  • Habitat: Tundra, alpine meadows and coastlines; Northern Hemisphere (Source)

The Brown Bear is one of the largeest terrestrial carnivores. Males and females vary in size by up to as much as 30% with most males weighing up to 350kg and most females weighing up tp 200kg.
